The Problem

AI passes the test.
Then fails the job.

That gap is what I've built my research and my company around. It's not a niche academic puzzle — it's the core trust crisis of our moment.

Lab → Deployment
Benchmarks lie.
AI systems ace evaluations designed for labs, then break in ways nobody measured. We're testing for what's easy, not what matters.
Synthetic ≠ Fake
Identity is broken.
Generative AI has made it impossible to trust what you see, hear, or read online. Detection tools are chasing symptoms. Nobody's fixing the system.
Policies ≠ Practice
Governance is theatre.
Regulated industries have thorough AI accountability documents and almost no AI accountability. The gap between what's written and what's deployed is wide — and growing.
Why me
I study the failures. I deploy into them.
Most people in this space do one or the other — study AI systems from the outside, or build them without looking at what breaks. I do both. I've spent years studying where and how AI systems fail trust. Now I'm deploying directly into those failure modes — which means my company has diagnostic depth most builders don't have, and my research has ground truth most academics can't access.
"I didn't stumble into this problem. I've spent years mapping exactly where AI breaks trust — and now I'm building the infrastructure to fix it."
