abstract: 'The rise of generative AI introduces unprecedented challenges for digital identity systems. Synthetic faces, voice clones, and text-based personas undermine assumptions embedded in identity verification pipelines built for a pre-generative-AI world. We taxonomize the threat landscape, survey existing mitigation approaches, and propose design principles for identity systems robust to generative AI attacks. We ground our analysis in case studies drawn from social media, financial services, and government identity programs.'
