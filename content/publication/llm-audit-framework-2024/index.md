abstract: 'We introduce a structured framework for auditing large language models (LLMs) across multiple dimensions of trustworthiness, including factual accuracy, calibration, robustness to adversarial prompts, demographic fairness, and alignment with human values. We apply the framework to evaluate six state-of-the-art LLMs and identify systematic gaps in current safety evaluation practices. Our work provides actionable guidance for practitioners and policymakers overseeing LLM deployments in high-stakes settings.'
