This project examines the governance challenges posed by AI automation in regulated industries. As AI systems take on increasingly consequential decisions in healthcare, financial services, and legal contexts, existing regulatory frameworks prove inadequate.

Key research questions:
- How should audit trails for AI-automated decisions be designed?
- What accountability mechanisms preserve meaningful human oversight?
- How do we govern AI automation across organizational and jurisdictional boundaries?
- What deployment standards reduce systematic risks from AI automation?

The project produces both empirical research (field studies, interviews) and normative work (governance frameworks, policy recommendations), with active engagement with regulatory bodies and standards organizations.
